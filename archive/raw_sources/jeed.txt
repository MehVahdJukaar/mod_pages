<p style="text-align: center;"><span style="font-size: 24px; font-family: helvetica, arial, sans-serif;"><strong>📖 About&nbsp;📖</strong></span></p>
<p style="text-align: left;"><br><span style="font-size: 18px;">Just Enough Effect Descriptions (JEED) is a JEI/REI/EMI plugin that provides useful information regarding status effects.</span></p>
<p style="text-align: left;">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="text-align: left;"><span style="font-size: 18px;"> The mod adds a new Effects recipe category accessed either by clicking any of the newly added status effect icons on the JEI item screen or by clicking an active effect from its box on the inventory screen.</span></p>
<p style="text-align: left;">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="text-align: left;"><span style="font-size: 18px;">Here you'll be able to view information regarding such status effect such as items that can provide it or its description as well as which mod it's from or its effect color. </span></p>
<p style="text-align: left;">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img src="https://i.imgur.com/P8qv5ZN.png" alt="" width="600" height="313"><img src="https://i.imgur.com/UfGgoGG.png" alt="" width="600" height="313"></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><span style="font-size: 14px;"><strong><img src="https://i.imgur.com/yXOxl9R.png" width="1688" height="42"></strong></span></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><span style="font-size: 24px;"><strong>🧪 Customization🧪</strong></span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: 18px;">The mod automatically adds all registered status effect to its page, however modded effects won't had a description.</span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><span style="font-size: 18px;">&nbsp;Anyone can easily add such descriptions to their effect by adding the string <span style="color: #843fa1;">"effect.[mod_id].[effect_name].description"</span> to their language file in their Resource Pack. Ideallu this should be done by modders themselves.</span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><span style="font-size: 18px;">As a Modded you can also use the <em>JeedAPI</em> class to register screen extension for your screen or vanilla classes to tell Jeed where effects are rendered to make them clickable</span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><span style="font-size: 18px;">Since the mod can only automatically detect food items as effect providing ones, mod developers can also easily add support to their custom effect or effect providing items by adding some recipes into their mod.</span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><span style="font-size: 18px;">To do so one can simply use one of the two newly added recipes categoris:</span></p>
<ul>
<li><span style="font-size: 18px;"><em>"jeed:effect_provider"</em>, which allows one to specify an item that can provide an effect in the following format:</span></li>
</ul>
<div class="spoiler">
<pre>{<br>  "type": "jeed:effect_provider",<br>  "effect":  "minecraft:haste",<br>  "providers": [<br>    {<br>      "item": "minecraft:beacon"<br>    }<br>  ]<br>}</pre>
<p>The Json can also contain the keys "fluid_providers" and "effect_providers", both of which can too hold a tag or list of fluids and effects respectively</p>
</div>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<ul>
<li><span style="font-size: 18px;"><em>"jeed:potion_provider"</em>, which allows one to specify an item that can contain any registered potion like tipped arrows for example:</span></li>
</ul>
<div class="spoiler">
<pre>{<br>  "type": "jeed:potion_provider",<br>  "providers": [<br>    {<br>      "item": "minecraft:splash_potion"<br>    },<br>    {<br>      "item": "minecraft:tipped_arrow"<br>    }<br>  ]<br>}</pre>
</div>
